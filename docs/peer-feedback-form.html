What you're reviewing

You are reviewing your peer's package design progress. This is constructive feedback to help them identify what's working and what needs attention before final artwork.

Complete both sections:

  1. Mark the rubric criteria (select one option per row)
  2. Write feedback (around 50 words total)

This should take about 10 minutes. Be specific and helpful.

Part 1: Rubric assessment

For each criterion, select the option that best describes your peer's current progress.

1. Required elements present
Product title, brand elements, barcode, ingredients/mandatory info are all included
2. Layout and hierarchy
Information is organised logically, with clear visual hierarchy
3. Design cohesion
Typography, colour and imagery work together coherently
4. Brand alignment
Design reflects Sportsgirl's brand identity appropriately
5. Technical setup
Appears to have correct resolution, colour mode, layers organised

Part 2: Written feedback

Write brief feedback (around 50 words or one paragraph) that identifies:

  • At least one strength in what you see
  • One thing they could try (a technique, specific tutorial, or course page reference)
Be specific. Instead of "good colours," try "the mint and coral palette creates strong contrast and fits the Sportsgirl aesthetic."

Helpful suggestions

If relevant, you could point your peer to:

  • Course notes: Pages on typography, colour systems, layout principles, or technical setup
  • YouTube tutorials: Name the topic (e.g., "improving type hierarchy in InDesign," "working with spot colours")
  • Techniques: Different approaches to hierarchy, colour contrast, composition or grid systems
  • Examples: High-pass student work or reference materials from class
