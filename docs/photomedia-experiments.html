Experiments generator

Try something new with your AT3 composite

Use this once your composition is roughly in place and you want to push the work further. Experiments fall into two categories: compositional (trying different images or arrangements) and technique (applying Photoshop skills to enhance what you have). Both count — the aim is to document what you tried and what you learned. Work non-destructively: use adjustment layers clipped to individual layers, or work on a stamped copy of your composite (⇧⌥⌘E).

On AI tools: Using built-in Photoshop AI to enhance existing photos is fine — adding a shadow, adjusting colour, cleaning up a background area. What is not OK is using AI to generate a whole new image, or sourcing AI-generated images from Adobe Stock. Watch what AI does to your photos: it can distort textures, smooth away detail, or produce patches that look inconsistent with the surrounding image. The actual photos need to show through.

You need to document two experiments for your AT3d Report. Save each experiment as a screenshot or JPEG. Write three sentences: what you tried, what you hoped to achieve, and why it did or did not work.
